## 1. Purpose

1.1 To establish a public–private partnership framework under which the Parties will jointly deploy, promote, and commercialise **EconomicBridge** — a multi-tenant Earth-Observation (EO) intelligence platform providing per-LGA poverty mapping, farmland/encroachment protection, crop-health monitoring, flood & drought early warning, aid coordination, economic mobility, and skills intelligence across Nigeria and, subsequently, ECOWAS member states.

1.2 The intended outcomes are: (a) adoption of EconomicBridge by federal ministries, departments, agencies and state governments; (b) improved data-driven decision-making in food security, disaster response and economic planning; and (c) a commercially sustainable Nigerian EO capability.

## 2. Scope of cooperation

2.1 **The Partner** shall, subject to its statutory mandate:
&nbsp;&nbsp;a) facilitate introductions and demonstrations of EconomicBridge to relevant Federal Government bodies, parastatals and state governments;
&nbsp;&nbsp;b) provide institutional endorsement and, where agreed, co-branding of deployments;
&nbsp;&nbsp;c) support procurement processes strictly in accordance with applicable law (including the Public Procurement Act and, for PPP arrangements, ICRC guidelines);
&nbsp;&nbsp;d) [optional: contribute satellite data, ground-truth data, or technical validation as specified in Schedule 2].

2.2 **Bizra** shall:
&nbsp;&nbsp;a) operate, maintain and continuously develop the EconomicBridge platform, including satellite ingestion (Copernicus Sentinel-1/2, NASA FIRMS, VIIRS, WorldPop and successors), ML models and alerting;
&nbsp;&nbsp;b) deliver demonstrations, pilots and deployments to institutions introduced under this MOU;
&nbsp;&nbsp;c) provide training and capacity-building for adopting institutions;
&nbsp;&nbsp;d) maintain hosting, security and data-protection standards as described in Clause 6.

## 3. Commercial framework

3.1 The Parties intend that revenues from adoptions enabled by the Partner shall be shared in accordance with a Definitive Agreement to be negotiated in good faith, guided by the EconomicBridge Revenue-Share Framework, with the Partner's share payable **to the Partner institution** and calculated on **net collected revenue**.

3.2 An indicative band of **[25–35]% of net revenue** on contracts the Partner sources and facilitates is acknowledged as the Parties' starting position, subject to the Definitive Agreement.

3.3 Nothing in this MOU creates any payment obligation. No payment of any kind shall be made to any individual official of either Party.

## 4. Intellectual property

4.1 All intellectual property in the EconomicBridge platform — software, models, pipelines, documentation and derived datasets — is and remains the exclusive property of Bizra.

4.2 The Partner is granted no licence except as expressly set out in a Definitive Agreement. Any data contributed by the Partner remains the Partner's property, licensed to Bizra for the purposes of this cooperation.

## 5. Non-exclusivity

5.1 This MOU is non-exclusive. Either Party may enter similar arrangements with third parties, save that a Definitive Agreement may provide territory- or sector-limited exclusivity tied to minimum revenue commitments.

## 6. Data protection & sovereignty

6.1 Bizra shall process Nigerian government data in compliance with the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 and applicable regulations; tenant data is isolated per institution; personally identifiable information is processed only under a signed Data Processing Agreement.

6.2 Government tenant data shall not be transferred outside jurisdictions permitted by the adopting institution's data policies.

## 7. Compliance & anti-corruption

7.1 Each Party warrants compliance with the Corrupt Practices and Other Related Offences Act, EFCC Act and all applicable procurement law. No Party shall offer, give or receive any inducement in connection with this MOU. Breach voids this MOU immediately.

## 8. Confidentiality

8.1 Each Party shall keep confidential all non-public information received under this MOU and use it solely for the purposes of this cooperation. This clause survives termination by three (3) years.

## 9. Governance

9.1 The Parties shall establish a Steering Committee of two (2) representatives each, meeting at least quarterly, to review pipeline, deployments and any disputes.

## 10. Status, term & termination

10.1 **This MOU is not legally binding**, except Clauses 4 (IP), 7 (Compliance) and 8 (Confidentiality), which bind the Parties.

10.2 Term: two (2) years from signature, renewable in writing. Either Party may terminate on thirty (30) days' written notice.

10.3 The Parties shall negotiate in good faith towards a Definitive Agreement within [90] days of signature.

## 11. Governing law

11.1 This MOU is governed by the laws of the Federal Republic of Nigeria.
