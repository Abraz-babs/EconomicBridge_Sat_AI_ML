## 1. Market context (why these prices)

The global Earth-Observation (EO) data & analytics market is worth **~$4–5 billion (2024)** and growing at **15–20% per year**, with the fastest growth in *downstream analytics* — turning imagery into decisions — precisely EconomicBridge's layer. Comparable pricing worldwide:

| Comparable | Typical government price point |
|---|---|
| International EO analytics SaaS (Planet-partner analytics, agri-monitoring platforms) | $50,000–$500,000+/yr per government client |
| Global satellite-monitoring dashboards for a single ministry | $100,000–$1M/yr |
| Consulting-built one-off EO studies | $50,000–$250,000 per study |

EconomicBridge's advantage: **local-first, multi-module, sovereign-friendly, and 30–70% below international vendors** — while covering seven decision domains (poverty, aid, farmland protection, crop health, disaster, mobility, skills) in one platform, per-LGA, with auditable satellite provenance (Sentinel-1/2, NASA FIRMS, VIIRS Black Marble, WorldPop).

## 4. Standard commercial terms

- **Billing:** annual in advance (government POs: 50% on signature, 50% at go-live acceptance, net-30).
- **Pilot pricing:** 3-month paid pilot at 25% of annual tier price, creditable against year-1 licence.
- **Multi-year:** 10% discount on 2-yr, 15% on 3-yr prepaid.
- **Public-sector escalation:** prices reviewed annually; capped at US CPI + 3%.
- **What's never discounted to zero:** ingestion compute + SMS pass-through costs.

## 5. Indicative value anchors (for negotiation)

- One prevented farmer–herder clash: conflict losses in Nigeria's middle belt are estimated in **billions of USD annually** — a $36k state licence is <0.01% of one state's exposure.
- One pre-positioned flood response (48–72h early warning): saves multiples of the licence in emergency logistics alone.
- Crop-stress early warning across 447 LGAs replaces field-survey costs that exceed **$1M/yr** at national scale.
